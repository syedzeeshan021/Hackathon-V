# Hardware Requirements

This section outlines the hardware recommendations and requirements for participating in the course, along with alternatives.

## Recommended Workstation

*   **Processor**: Intel Core i7 (8th Gen or newer) / AMD Ryzen 7 (2nd Gen or newer)
*   **RAM**: 16GB or more
*   **Storage**: 500GB SSD or more
*   **Graphics Card**: NVIDIA GPU (GTX 1060 or equivalent/newer) for simulation and AI workloads, with at least 6GB VRAM. (Required for NVIDIA Isaac platform)
*   **Operating System**: Ubuntu 20.04 LTS (recommended), or Windows 10/11 with WSL2.

## Edge Kits (Optional)

For hands-on experience with physical robots:

*   **NVIDIA Jetson Nano/Xavier NX**: For small-scale AI and robotics projects.
*   **Raspberry Pi 4**: For basic robotics control and IoT integration.

## Robot Options (Optional)

*   **Clearpath Robotics Jackal/Warthog**: For advanced outdoor mobile robotics research.
*   **Unitree Go1/Go2**: For quadrupedal locomotion research.
*   **OpenMANIPULATOR-X**: For robotic arm manipulation tasks.

## Cloud Alternatives

If you do not have access to the recommended hardware, cloud-based solutions can be utilized:

*   **AWS EC2 with GPU instances**: For running simulations and AI models.
*   **Google Cloud Platform (GCP) AI Platform**: For machine learning development.
*   **Azure Machine Learning**: For cloud-based AI workloads.
*   **Google Colab/Kaggle Notebooks**: For smaller-scale AI experiments.

## Software Requirements

*   **ROS 2 Humble Hawksbill**
*   **Gazebo** (Ignition or Classic)
*   **Python 3.8+**
*   **Docker**
*   **Git**
*   **Visual Studio Code** (or preferred IDE)
