# Weekly Breakdown

This section outlines the topics and activities planned for each week of the course.

## Week 1: Introduction to Physical AI & Robotics
*   Course overview, introduction to Physical AI concepts
*   History and future of humanoid robotics
*   Introduction to ROS 2

## Week 2: ROS 2 Fundamentals
*   ROS 2 workspace setup, nodes, topics, services
*   Command line tools and debugging
*   Basic robot control with ROS 2

## Week 3: Robot Kinematics & Dynamics
*   Forward and inverse kinematics
*   Joint control and trajectory generation
*   Introduction to robot dynamics

## Week 4: Simulation with Gazebo
*   Setting up Gazebo environment
*   Creating URDF models for robots
*   Interfacing ROS 2 with Gazebo simulations

## Week 5: Vision Systems for Robots
*   Introduction to computer vision
*   Camera models, image processing
*   Object detection and tracking

## Week 6: Navigation & Localization
*   SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)
*   Path planning algorithms
*   Robot navigation stacks

## Week 7: Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)
*   Safety in HRI
*   Gesture recognition, speech command processing
*   Collaborative robotics

## Week 8: Machine Learning for Robotics
*   Introduction to reinforcement learning
*   Deep learning in robotics
*   Learning from demonstration

## Week 9: NVIDIA Isaac & AI Platforms
*   Overview of NVIDIA Isaac ecosystem
*   Using Isaac SDK for robotics development
*   GPU-accelerated perception and simulation

## Week 10: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Integration
*   Connecting vision, language, and action models
*   Large Language Models (LLMs) in robotics
*   Semantic understanding for robot tasks

## Week 11: Humanoid Robot Control
*   Balance and gait generation
*   Whole-body control
*   Advanced locomotion techniques

## Week 12: Ethical & Societal Implications
*   Bias in AI, job displacement
*   Safety and accountability of autonomous systems
*   Future of Physical AI and human society

## Week 13: Project Work & Presentations
*   Student project development
*   Final project presentations and demonstrations
*   Course review and feedback
