# Assessments

This course employs a variety of assessment methods to evaluate student learning and comprehension.

## Components

### 1. Weekly Quizzes (30%)
*   Short online quizzes covering material from lectures and readings.
*   Designed to test fundamental understanding and retention of key concepts.
*   Administered through the learning management system.

### 2. Programming Assignments (40%)
*   Hands-on coding tasks involving ROS 2, Gazebo, and other robotics tools.
*   Focus on implementing algorithms for robot control, perception, and navigation.
*   Assignments will be submitted and graded through a code submission platform.

### 3. Final Project (30%)
*   A culminating project where students will design, implement, and demonstrate a Physical AI or humanoid robotics application.
*   Can be an extension of a previous assignment, a novel application, or a research-oriented exploration.
*   Includes a project proposal, progress report, final presentation, and a written report.
*   Students are encouraged to work in teams (max 3 members).

## Grading Policy

*   Grading will be based on correctness, efficiency, code quality, and originality for programming assignments and the final project.
*   Late submissions for quizzes and assignments will incur penalties as outlined in the course syllabus.
*   Academic honesty is strictly enforced.
